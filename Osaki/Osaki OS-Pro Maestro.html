The Osaki Pro Maestro offers a unique and advanced massage experience that can provide relief for those suffering from muscle pain or tension. With its advanced technology and customizable features, it offers a convenient and effective way to relax and unwind in the comfort of your own home.

4D Massage Mechanism
The 4D massage allows another experience with account for the speed and rhythm of the massage. Massages are adjustable with 8 4D adjustments, 5 Strength adjustments, 5 Intensity adjustments, and 5 speed adjustments for the massage rollers.
Kneading
Calf Massage
The calf kneading motion will increase the blood flow of the legs, helping them to reach a higher level of relaxation. On the upper section of the leg rest, the air massagers will rotate in a circular motion to add a new dimension to the calf massage.
Body Scan Technology
The OS-Pro Maestro will perform a body scan to map the key areas of the neck and back to ensure the optimal amount of pressure applied during the massage. The scanning technology will detect your shoulders height, which can be re-calibrated once the scan is complete.
SL-track Technology
The SL-Track is made up of Laser-Crafted Steel. The track allows the massage rollers to cover the entirety of your body. The SL-Track reaches all the way from your neck and shoulders to your hamstrings.
Zero Gravity Reclining
and Space Saving
Inspired by NASA, the Zero Gravity position is the perfect position for ultimate relaxation and helping maximize the intensity of the massage. With Osaki’s Space Saving Technology, you may set the chair 5 inches from the wall and still achieve a complete recline.

The Osaki OS-4D Pro Maestro offers a total of 32 airbags in the massage chair. This compression massage helps to continue increasing blood circulation throughout the body to relieve pain and tension.

Measuring Guide
  • Calculate the doorway width by subtracting 2 inches from the smallest inner width of the door frame. (1 inch from each side)
  • Please use a standard measuring tape to measure the doorway.
  • If the door cannot be fully opened, open it as much as possible and measure the width from the edge of the door hinge.
  • If there is a rounded curve on the wall, calculate the inner width based on the most protruding point of the wall.
