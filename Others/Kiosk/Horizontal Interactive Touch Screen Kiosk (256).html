What does a Horizontal Interactive Kiosk do?
Everything explained in plain language - no tech jargon required.
Ergonomic Landscape Viewing
Designed with a tilted horizontal screen that makes it incredibly comfortable for users to view complex information, interactive maps, or detailed architectural plans without straining their neck.
Runs Windows Apps Natively
Powered by a robust Intel i5 processor running Windows 11 Pro. Easily install custom software suites, 3D modeling tools, or complex web applications without any compatibility issues.
Stunning 4K Clarity
Bring content to life with an ultra-sharp 4K (3840 x 2160) Grade-A LG Panel. Crisp, bright visuals ensure that even the finest details in blueprints or product catalogs are highly legible.
Highly Responsive Touch
Utilizing advanced multi-touch technology, users can zoom, swipe, pinch, and annotate just like they would on a massive tablet, allowing for highly interactive presentations and self-service browsing.
Effortless Upgrades (OPS Module)
Features an Intel OPS (Open Pluggable Specification) design. The computing core can easily slide out for maintenance, upgrades, or quick servicing without taking down the entire kiosk.
178° Wide Viewing Angle
Perfect for collaboration. The ultra-wide viewing angle ensures that colors stay true and images remain sharp even when multiple people gather around the kiosk to view content together.
